Frequently Asked Questions

Got questions about exploring Kenya with us? Check out our FAQs below or contact us directly for assistance.

Kenya offers a plethora of breathtaking destinations. Some must-visit places include:

  • The iconic Masai Mara for unforgettable safaris.
  • The stunning beaches of Diani and Watamu.
  • The picturesque landscapes of Mount Kenya and the Great Rift Valley.
  • The vibrant culture of Nairobi, the capital city.

There's no shortage of exciting activities to experience in Kenya:

  • Embark on thrilling safaris to spot the Big Five and witness the Great Migration.
  • Relax on pristine beaches, go snorkeling, or indulge in water sports.
  • Hike to scenic viewpoints, explore nature reserves, and visit cultural villages.
  • Participate in hot air balloon rides, camel treks, and guided nature walks.

Your safety and well-being are our top priorities. Here's what we do to ensure a safe travel experience:

  • Partner with reputable accommodations and transportation providers adhering to strict safety protocols.
  • Provide knowledgeable guides who are trained in first aid and emergency procedures.
  • Offer comprehensive travel insurance options for added peace of mind.
  • Maintain open communication channels to address any concerns or emergencies promptly.

Kenya boasts a lively nightlife scene with something for everyone:

  • Explore Nairobi's bustling bars, clubs, and live music venues.
  • Experience the vibrant coastal nightlife in Mombasa and Malindi.
  • Enjoy themed parties, cultural performances, and outdoor events.
  • Discover hidden gems in local neighborhoods and entertainment districts.

Kenya's farmers markets offer a delightful array of fresh produce, crafts, and local delicacies:

  • Visit Nairobi's Maasai Market for traditional crafts, jewelry, and souvenirs.
  • Explore the organic offerings at Karen Blixen Coffee Garden and Cottages.
  • Experience the vibrant atmosphere of local markets in towns and villages across Kenya.
  • Engage with farmers and artisans, sample regional specialties, and support local businesses.

Watamu is renowned for its excellent kite surfing conditions, offering enthusiasts:

  • Consistent wind patterns and expansive beaches perfect for kite surfing.
  • Professional kite surfing schools and rental facilities for all skill levels.
  • Breathtaking scenery, warm waters, and abundant marine life for unforgettable sessions.
  • Opportunities for kite surfing competitions, events, and community gatherings.

Lamu offers an enchanting setting for dhow sailing adventures, with:

  • Historic dhow cruises along Lamu Archipelago's pristine coastline and islands.
  • Captivating sunset and moonlight sails amidst tranquil waters and starlit skies.
  • Opportunities to explore hidden coves, mangrove forests, and remote beaches.
  • Authentic cultural experiences, delicious onboard cuisine, and warm Swahili hospitality.
